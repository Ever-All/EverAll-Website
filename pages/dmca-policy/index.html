DMCA Policy

Last Updated: May 17, 2025

EverAll respects intellectual property rights and expects its users to do the same. This DMCA (Digital Millennium Copyright Act) policy outlines procedures for reporting copyright infringement and how we handle such claims.

1. Reporting Copyright Infringement

If you believe your copyrighted work has been used on our platform without authorization, please submit a written notification that includes:

  • Physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent
  • Description of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed
  • Description of where the allegedly infringing material is located on our service
  • Your contact information (address, phone number, email)
  • Statement of good faith belief that the use is not authorized
  • Statement that the information is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner

3. Counter-Notification

If you believe your content was wrongly removed, you may submit a counter-notification including:

  • Your physical or electronic signature
  • Identification of the removed material and its previous location
  • Statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the material was wrongly removed
  • Your consent to local federal court jurisdiction
  • Your name, address, and phone number

4. Repeat Infringer Policy

We maintain a policy to terminate account privileges of repeat infringers. A user who receives multiple copyright strikes may have their account suspended or terminated.

5. Good Faith Belief

We process DMCA notices in good faith and strive to follow the law while protecting both copyright holders and users' rights. False claims may lead to liability under Section 512(f) of the DMCA.

6. Modifications to Content

We may modify, prevent access to, or remove any content that we believe infringes copyrights, with or without notice.

7. Legal Jurisdiction and Compliance

All DMCA claims and counter-claims are processed under United States law. By submitting a DMCA notice or counter-notice, you consent to:

  • United States federal court jurisdiction for DMCA-related disputes
  • Service of process via the email address you provide
  • Application of United States copyright law to your claim

Our DMCA process applies only to copyright claims. For other legal issues:
