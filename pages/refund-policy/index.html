Refund Policy

Last Updated: May 11, 2025

1. Subscription Refunds

EverAll's refund policy is strictly limited to new subscribers within their first 14 days:

  • Only first-time subscribers are eligible for refunds
  • Refund requests must be made within 14 days of initial purchase
  • Refund amount will be the subscription cost minus all payment processing fees
  • After the 14-day period, all subscription payments become strictly non-refundable
  • Refunds are processed through the original payment method

2. Non-Refundable Payments

The following are strictly non-refundable:

  • All subscription renewal payments
  • Any subscription payments after the initial 14-day period
  • Payment processing fees from any transaction
  • Partial months of service used
  • Additional services or add-ons purchased separately
  • Accounts suspended for Terms of Service violations

3. Subscription Renewal Terms

By continuing your subscription beyond the initial 14-day period, you acknowledge and agree that:

  • All future subscription payments are non-refundable
  • Cancellations will stop future renewals but will not result in refunds
  • You maintain access until the end of your current billing period
  • Early cancellation does not entitle you to a partial refund

4. Cancellation Process

To cancel your subscription and request a refund:

  • Log into your EverAll account
  • Navigate to Subscription Settings
  • Select "Cancel Subscription"
  • Choose "Request Refund" if within the eligible period

5. Processing Time

Approved refunds are typically processed within 5-10 business days. The exact timing depends on your payment provider and banking institution.

6. Special Circumstances

We may consider refunds outside the standard policy in cases of:

  • Extended service outages
  • Technical issues preventing service access
  • Billing errors or duplicate charges

7. Subscription Continuation

Canceling your subscription will:

  • Stop automatic renewals
  • Maintain access until the current billing period ends
  • Not affect access to free features
